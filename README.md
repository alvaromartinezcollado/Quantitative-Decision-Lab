# Quantitative Decision Lab

## Quantitative Risk & Decision Intelligence Platform

Quantitative Decision Lab is an end-to-end quantitative analytics project designed to model risk, uncertainty and decision-making in complex financial and industrial systems.

The project combines financial data engineering, statistical modelling, risk analytics, Monte Carlo simulation, optimisation and interactive dashboarding to support better decisions under uncertainty.

## Project Objective

The goal of this project is to build a professional-grade quantitative decision platform that can:

* collect, clean and structure financial and business-related data;
* analyse asset behaviour, volatility, correlations and risk factors;
* compute portfolio risk and performance metrics;
* simulate uncertain scenarios using Monte Carlo methods;
* evaluate decisions under different market and business conditions;
* optimise allocations, strategies or resource decisions;
* present results through an interactive dashboard and consulting-style reports.

## Motivation

Modern companies and financial institutions operate under uncertainty. Market conditions, interest rates, supply chains, demand, costs and operational constraints can all affect strategic decisions.

This project aims to apply engineering, statistics, data science and quantitative finance to build structured tools for risk analysis and decision support.

## Planned Modules

### 1. Market Data Pipeline

Financial data ingestion, cleaning and transformation.

### 2. Cross-Asset Risk Analytics

Computation of individual asset risk and return metrics, including volatility, drawdowns, Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, VaR and CVaR.

### 3. Portfolio Construction & Allocation

Construction of different portfolio allocations using selected assets and predefined weights.

This module analyses how asset weights affect portfolio returns, volatility and diversification.

### 4. Portfolio Risk Analytics

Computation of portfolio-level risk and performance metrics, including portfolio return, volatility, drawdown, Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, VaR and CVaR.

### 5. Scenario Analysis & Stress Testing

Evaluation of how portfolios behave under adverse scenarios such as market crashes, interest rate shocks, energy shocks, sector drawdowns and volatility spikes.

### 6. Monte Carlo Simulation

Simulation of uncertain future outcomes using stochastic methods.

### 7. Decision Optimisation

Optimisation of portfolio allocations and decision variables under risk and uncertainty.

### 8. Strategy Backtesting

Testing and comparison of systematic decision rules such as momentum, mean reversion, risk parity and volatility-based allocation.

### 9. Performance Optimisation

Benchmarking and optimisation of computationally intensive modules using vectorisation, Numba and potentially GPU acceleration.

### 10. Dashboard

Interactive dashboard for visualising portfolio performance, risk metrics, scenario results, Monte Carlo simulations and decision recommendations.
